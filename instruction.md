# Writing Instructions

## Strong Definitions For Emotions

- A weak definition just swaps one vague word for another.
- A strong definition says what the feeling means, what causes it, and what makes it different from nearby moods.
- Definitions should explain the core feeling, not just list synonyms.
- For heavier moods like `abused`, `betrayed`, or `ashamed`, include the power dynamic or source of harm when relevant.
- Good definition pattern:
  `what the feeling is + why it happens + what makes it distinct`

## Strong Examples For Emotions

- A weak example says what happened.
- A strong example says when, how big, who had power, and why it is shocking.
- The best examples feel like the opening paragraph of a magazine feature, not a classroom sentence.
- Prefer concrete facts such as year, numbers, public figures, institutions, money, scale, and visible damage.
- The `example` field must be real, not invented.
- Every example must point to a real article, book, or story that the example comes from.
- Save the source URL in `example_link`.
- If the source is a book or story, use a stable public page for that work.
- Use examples tied to year 2010 or earlier only.
- Do not use politics or politician figures in examples.
- For `abused`, the best examples usually have:
  `year + institution/person + number + betrayal of trust + damage`
